The Third Involution

x → x/(x−1)  ·  a projective construction

Working notes  ·  K. Forseth  ·  May 2026

The projective line admits exactly six Möbius transformations that permute the three special points {0, 1, ∞} among themselves. These six form the anharmonic group, isomorphic to S3. Three are involutions (order 2); two are 3-cycles (order 3); one is the identity.

This set of working notes examines the structural difference among the three involutions through geometric construction. The central finding: Complement and Reciprocal each require only one axis for their construction, while the third involution — x → x/(x−1) — requires both axes and a mediating curve. That dependence is not asserted but demonstrated: the hinge point (1, 1) of the construction is forced by the intersection of the unit hyperbola xy = 1 with the diagonal x = y.

The notes also establish why the two 3-cycles have no real fixed points: their fixed-point equation x2−x+1=0 encodes the incompatibility between the additive and multiplicative registers over the reals — the same incompatibility that makes the third involution structurally dependent on both.

Key findings
I
Minimal apparatus distinguishes the three involutions. Complement requires one axis and a compass (additive construction). Reciprocal requires one axis and a straightedge (projective construction). The third involution x → x/(x−1) requires both axes, both instruments, and a mediating curve.
II
The hinge (1,1) is not chosen — it is found. The unit hyperbola xy = 1 intersects the diagonal x = y at exactly one point: (1,1). This intersection is forced by the joint presence of both axes. Remove either axis and the hinge vanishes.
III
The 3-cycles have no real fixed points. A real fixed point of the composition would require a pair {x,y} satisfying both x+y=1 (Complement's 2-cycle condition) and xy=1 (Reciprocal's 2-cycle condition) simultaneously. This requires arithmetic mean ½ < geometric mean 1, which violates AM ≥ GM for positive reals. No such pair exists on the real line.
IV
x2−x+1=0 encodes the incompatibility. The roots e±iπ/3 are where both conditions are satisfied simultaneously — but only in the complex plane. The quotient ring ℤ[x]/(x2−x+1) is not imported: it is the forced habitat of the fixed points the two generators jointly require.
