Fig. 6 — Reciprocal: x → 1/x

What this diagram shows: Reciprocal is a purely projective construction. It requires only a single line with one marked point — the unit 1 — and a straightedge. No compass, no distance measurement, no second axis. The unit is treated as a ratio: the multiplicative identity.

The construction finds 1/x as the harmonic conjugate of x with respect to {−1, 1}. Two freely chosen auxiliary points S and T above the line generate a network of straightedge lines (shown in blue). Their intersections U and V determine a line (shown in red) that meets the axis at exactly 1/x. The result is independent of where S and T are chosen — any position gives the same answer.

Why −1 and 1? The harmonic conjugate of x with respect to {−1, 1} equals 1/x because these are the fixed points of the Reciprocal transformation itself. The construction uses the transformation's own fixed points as reference — which is exactly the projective structure the transformation inhabits.

The fixed point 1 never moves: 1/1 = 1. Drag x toward 0 and watch 1/x escape to ∞. Drag x toward ∞ and watch 1/x approach 0. The 0↔∞ exchange is enacted before your eyes.

Minimal apparatus: one line, one marked point (the unit 1), a straightedge. No compass, no distance, no second axis. This is an entirely projective construction — it lives in the multiplicative world.

Construction: harmonic conjugate of x with respect to {−1,  1}
  1. Choose auxiliary point S above the line (freely)
  2. Choose point T on line S – x (freely, between S and x)
  3. Draw line T – (−1); it meets line S – 1 at point U
  4. Draw line T – 1; it meets line S – (−1) at point V
  5. Line U – V meets the axis at 1/x
The construction is purely projective — straightedge only, no compass, no distance measurement.
Drag along the line to move x. S and T are fixed auxiliary points; only the construction lines change. The fixed point 1 never moves: 1/1 = 1.
